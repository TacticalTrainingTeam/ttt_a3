# Field Manual (Plain Markdown)

This file mirrors the field manual hint content in plain Markdown format.

## JTAC / CAS

### JTAC CAS Quick Guide

- **Before tasking aircraft**
  - Open channel and request **Check-In**: aircraft type, current position, weapons, **TOS**
  - Define **IP/BP** and expected **holding area**
  - Know own positions and no-fire constraints before any attack run
- **Build and send 9-Liner**
  - (1) IP/BP
  - (2) Heading
  - (3) Distance
  - (4) Elevation
  - (5) Description
  - (6) Location
  - (7) Type Mark
  - (8) Friendlies
  - (9) Egress
  - State weapon restrictions in **Remarks** when required
- **Marking and friendlies**
  - Use clear marks (**Laser** / **Smoke** / None) and tell the pilot exactly what to expect
  - For **Danger Close**, own troops must be clearly marked and reported
  - If no own troops are relevant: report **No Factor**
- **Attack control calls**
  - Pilot at IP/BP: **Inbound** → respond with **Continue** or **Abort**
  - Pilot sees mark: **Spot**, cannot identify: **No Spot**
  - Pilot ready to fire: **Tally**, then JTAC gives **Cleared Hot**
  - If no clearance is given, attack is aborted via egress
- **After effects / next run**
  - Give quick **BDA** (Hit / Miss, remaining threats)
  - Decide **Reattack** or hold at IP/BP
  - Update 9-Liner whenever target, friendlies, mark or weapon plan changes

**Golden rule:** **No visual identification, no release.**

## FO / FDCB

### FO Quick Guide

- **Before calling fire**
  - Clarify mission intent with leadership and define desired effect
  - Select firing position(s), resupply status and comms with gun line
  - Confirm map/grid system and elevation source used by both sides
- **Send FDCB (7 lines)**
  - (1) Type: **I** = fire on readiness, **II** = fire on command, **III** = timed impact
  - (2) Attack: point / line / area
  - (3) Coordinate: minimum 8-digit
  - (4) Elevation (ASL)
  - (5) Round count
  - (6) Ammunition type
  - (7) Remarks
  - Number fire missions so repeats and sequencing are clear
- **Adjustments and control**
  - Observe impact and correct quickly (direction / range / add-drop)
  - Use Type II when you need controlled release timing
  - Use remarks for timing and sequencing (e.g. simultaneous or delayed execution)
- **Ammo and effect choice**
  - **HE** for general effects and hard suppression
  - **Proximity/airburst** for infantry in open terrain
  - **Smoke** for screening or marking
  - **Illumination** for night visibility
- **Safety and follow-up**
  - Never fire without confirmed friendlies/no-fire awareness around target area
  - After each volley: report effect, request **Re-Attack** if needed, send updated situation
  - Stop or shift fire immediately when friendly movement changes

**Golden rule:** **Precision first, volume second.**

## Sniper Team

### Sniper Team Quick Guide

- **Mission profile and route plan**
  - (1) Define objective: observation, HVT removal, corridor security or support
  - (2) Plan infiltrate and exfiltrate routes away from roads/open terrain
  - (3) Prepare fallback position and emergency extraction option
- **Build firing position**
  - Use concealment and offset from obvious loopholes/windows
  - Shooter and spotter keep separate fields of view and clear sectors
  - Prepare immediate relocation route before first shot
- **Recon and target preparation**
  - Name positions/targets consistently and set target priority with command intent
  - Collect stable reference data (range, azimuth, holds, behavior) per target
  - Prefer measuring static reference objects near moving targets
- **Engagement workflow (spotter-led)**
  - (1) Spotter confirms target and readiness call
  - (2) Shooter confirms visual and hold data
  - (3) Spotter gives fire command, then immediate correction or follow-up
  - (4) Confirm effect (hit/miss/neutralized) and update priority list
- **After shot: move or stay**
  - If compromised or counter-recon risk rises: break contact and relocate
  - If still covert: continue observation and only re-engage by priority
  - Keep command informed with short, actionable updates

**Golden rule:** **Information gain and survivability are worth more than one extra shot.**

## Engineers / EOD

### Demining/EOD Quick Guide

- **5 C's on suspect explosive**
  - (1) **Confirm** with detector
  - (2) **Clear** distance from blast/fragment area
  - (3) **Call** EOD support
  - (4) **Cordon** and keep people/vehicles out
  - (5) **Control** until EOD reports area safe
- **Manual mine lane (opening a minefield)**
  - Leader defines lane start/end and security; minefields are treated as monitored
  - Engineers move as chain with spacing; detector scans front and side sectors
  - On contact: report, all others stop/go prone, finder disarms or clears mine
  - Mark lane edges continuously and keep route discipline
- **Tool usage essentials**
  - **Mine detector** in pistol slot; activate after equipping (deactivates on weapon/vehicle change)
  - Detector range is short; advance slowly and read delayed audio cue carefully
  - **Disarm kit** required for ACE disarm: approach prone, empty hands, disarm via ACE interaction
  - Some mines must **not** be disarmed (e.g. SLAM, bouncing mine): clear by shot or controlled charge
  - **Spray cans** for lane marks via ACE self interaction; use standard color discipline
  - **Wirecutter** opens fences with ACE interaction when obstacle removal is needed
- **Fast clearance options**
  - MICLIC clears an area of about **70m length x 4m width**
  - After activation there is about a **45 second delay** before detonation
  - Safety: move at least **45m** away and into hard cover before blast
  - ED-1D UGV can detect and clear from distance; use correct ammo mode for mine type

**Golden rule:** **Slow search, clear comms, strict marking, no shortcuts.**

## Logistics

### Logistics Quick Guide

- **Core priorities**
  - **Self-protection first**, then **people before material**
  - Logistics avoids combat and aborts when transport risk is too high
  - Keep material tracked and protected against loss or misuse
- **5-Liner for every order**
  - (0) Number
  - (1) Location
  - (2) Heading
  - (3) Receiver
  - (4) Task
  - (5) Remarks/Marking
  - Transmit with pauses, require readback, fix gaps immediately
  - Use stable place names for whole mission; new location means new name
- **Execution modes**
  - Ground: delivery with handover, with remain, or forward staging point
  - Air: primarily handover only, minimum time on ground, abort if LZ not ready
  - Mixed: air to LZ, then ground from LZ to VP near own troops
- **Radio and handover discipline**
  - Open supply net at mission start and confirm readiness/material status
  - On approach, announce in time so receiver secures VP/LZ and marks properly
  - After handover, report order completion and updated stock/location status
- **Practical load management**
  - Use standardized crate names/abbreviations (e.g. VBT, VBA, Inf Mun, Fhz Mun)
  - If available, use crate renaming and cargo-pack tools to reduce loading errors
  - Keep map/list overview so backup leader can continue without full recount

**Golden rule:** **Reliable process beats fast improvisation.**

## Air Mobile Infantry

### Air Mobile Infantry Quick Guide

- **Parachute preparation**
  - (1) Mark **WP**, **DZ** and at least one **SZ** on map
  - (2) Take parachute, altimeter and mission-specific equipment
  - (3) Set jump order, jump type and jump frequency before boarding
  - (4) Jumpmaster coordinates sequence; nobody exits without call
- **Jump execution**
  - Vehicle reference values matter: do not jump too low, too slow or too fast
  - Systemsprung: open immediately
  - HALO: open no later than 500m / 250m absolute last brake margin
  - HAHO: open a few seconds after exit and navigate from height
  - Keep spacing to avoid collisions with the man in front
- **Flight, landing, rally**
  - Use max glide speed until landing phase, then slow down in steps
  - From 50m: max about 5m/s
  - 20m before touchdown: slow to about 3m/s
  - Touchdown at about 2m/s or less to avoid injury
  - After landing: report on squad net, move to SZ, search missing teammates as ordered
- **ACE emergency note**
  - If main chute fails, cut it via ACE interaction and deploy reserve; reserve is not steerable

**Golden rule:** **Order, spacing and rally discipline matter more than speed.**

## Helicopter Infantry Transport

### Helicopter Infantry Quick Guide

- **Outside the aircraft**
  - Approach side-loading helicopters from the front, then arc to the side so crew keeps visual contact
  - Keep clear of weapons, rotors and sloped rotor disks; crew instructions override all other assumptions
  - On hot LZs, board fast and ignore normal seat order if speed is required
- **Inside the aircraft**
  - Keep internal chatter low; if radio is unavoidable, use whisper level
  - Infantry scans for threats, muzzle flashes, obstacles and informs pilot immediately
  - Nr. 2 can join the crew net when needed and reports when all elements are offloaded
- **LZ and information flow**
  - Clarify **where**, **what**, **how** and **who** before launch
  - FAC marks primary and alternate LZ and passes 5-Liner / updates to crew
  - Good LZ: early visible, enough space, as level as possible, with cover and few obstacles
  - Pilot may abort at any time if the LZ or approach is unsafe
- **Touchdown, fast-rope, lift-off**
  - After **Touchdown**, control of infantry returns to troop leader for dismount orders
  - Fast-rope: prepare system and lower ropes via ACE; rope out only on command; last man reports ground reached
  - After task completion or full accountability, pilot lifts off and leaves the LZ

**Golden rule:** **Safe approach, short ground time, clear crew-FAC-infantry handover.**
